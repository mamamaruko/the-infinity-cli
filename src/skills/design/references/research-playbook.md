# Research Playbook

## Goal

Collect enough high-signal references to make confident decisions without copying screens directly.

## Search Angles

For each task, explore at least 4 of these angles:

- Screen type: pricing, onboarding, settings, dashboard, empty state
- Industry context: fintech, devtools, ecommerce, healthcare, SaaS
- Interaction pattern: table filters, multi-step form, upgrade prompt, retention flow
- Emotional target: trust, urgency, clarity, calm, momentum
- Structural style: dense expert UI vs. guided low-friction flow

## Capture Format

For each reference, record:

- Source
- Pattern observed
- Why it works
- Risk if copied as-is
- Adaptation for our product

## Minimum Evidence

- Single screen: 8 references minimum
- Multi-step flow: 3 flow examples minimum
- 1 counter-example minimum (what failed and why)

## Stop Conditions

Stop research when all are true:

- You can explain 3 recurring patterns and 2 meaningful differences
- You know which pattern to keep and which to reject
- Additional references do not add new insight

## Common Failure Modes

- Collecting visuals but not interaction details
- Copying layout without understanding user intent
- Skipping counter-examples
- Ending research without a clear selection rationale
